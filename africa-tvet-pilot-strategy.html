The Challenge

Africa's Skills Gap Is a
Solvable Infrastructure Problem

Sub-Saharan Africa's manufacturing sector is expanding, yet TVET institutions struggle to deliver industry-aligned, technology-integrated training. The gap is not a lack of ambition — it is a lack of the right tools, qualified instructors, and adaptive content delivery at scale.

⚡

Infrastructure constraints

Unreliable power supply and limited lab equipment leave learners without hands-on exposure to modern industrial systems.

👩‍🏫

Instructor capacity gap

Qualified TVET trainers in smart manufacturing are scarce. When equipment exists, the expertise to teach with it often does not.

📋

Certification misalignment

Training programmes often lack alignment with recognised qualification frameworks (NQF, TEVETA, BTEC), limiting graduate employability.

🤝

Industry linkage deficit

TVET institutions rarely have structured pathways to manufacturing employers, leaving graduates without clear employment pipelines.

Target Beneficiaries

Who We Serve — and What They Need

This pilot targets four distinct beneficiary groups whose needs are interconnected. Solving for one without the others produces programmes that look good on paper but fail in practice. IEG's integrated approach addresses all four simultaneously.

🧑‍🔧

Young Job Seekers

Ages 18–30 · NEET population · school-to-work transition

Young people who have completed secondary education but cannot access formal employment because their skills do not match what manufacturers actually need on the factory floor.

Core unmet need Hands-on, industry-relevant technical training with a recognised qualification that employers trust — delivered in their own language and context.
👩‍🏫

TVET Instructors

Existing college staff · technical trainers · workshop facilitators

Instructors who are experienced educators but have not had access to modern smart factory equipment or digital pedagogy tools. Their knowledge is deep — their toolbox is outdated.

Core unmet need Practical upskilling on IEG equipment, AI-assisted teaching tools, and updated curriculum frameworks that align with current industry standards.
🏛️

TVET Institutions

Public TVET colleges · polytechnics · training centres

Institutions that want to modernise but face budget constraints, procurement complexity, and lack of technical implementation support. Equipment alone is not enough — they need a complete system.

Core unmet need A turnkey Living Lab model: equipment, curriculum, AI delivery platform, instructor training, and qualification accreditation — implemented as one integrated package.
🏭

Manufacturing Employers

Automotive · electronics · mining processing · light manufacturing

Companies hiring at volume who spend significant resources re-training TVET graduates because entry-level skills do not match production line requirements. They want reliable talent pipelines, not one-off recruits.

Core unmet need A predictable pipeline of graduates whose practical competencies are verified against real equipment — with qualifications they can trust without additional internal testing.
Country-Specific Intervention

What We Do, and Why It Matters Here

The same structural problems — skills mismatch, outdated equipment, no employer linkage — manifest differently in South Africa and Zambia. Each country receives a tailored intervention designed with local TVET experts, not imported wholesale from Korea.

🇿🇦

South Africa

Automotive · E&E
The local reality

South Africa has one of the continent's most developed TVET systems — 50 public colleges, an NQF framework, and active SETAs — yet youth unemployment exceeds 60%. The problem is not the absence of TVET; it is the disconnect between what colleges teach and what the automotive and electronics manufacturing sectors actually require. Employers report that graduates lack practical, equipment-level competency.

Why IEG's approach works here

South Africa's qualification infrastructure is mature enough to absorb an aligned intervention immediately. IEG equipment maps directly onto merSETA (Manufacturing, Engineering and Related Services SETA) unit standards. The AI layer (Emotii) addresses the instructor capacity shortage without requiring new hires. Most importantly, the co-design model ensures NQF Level 4 certification is embedded from day one — not retrofitted.

Specific interventions
  • Deploy IEG 3432 E&E workstations aligned to merSETA electrical unit standards
  • Install IEG 3200-M Smart Factory Trainer in one host college as a certified Living Lab
  • Co-author NQF Level 4 curriculum with local TVET experts and merSETA advisors
  • AI tutoring in English + isiZulu/Afrikaans to close language-access barriers
  • Structured employer placement programme with 3 anchor manufacturers in Gauteng / KZN
  • Instructor upskilling certification: 15 trainers through IEG Train-the-Trainer programme
🇿🇲

Zambia

Mining Process · Manufacturing
The local reality

Zambia's copper mining sector and adjacent manufacturing industry are expanding rapidly — but the TVET system, overseen by TEVETA, struggles to keep pace. Equipment in most colleges is 15–20 years old. Load-shedding (up to 14 hours daily in some regions) makes technology-dependent training unreliable. Instructors are willing but under-resourced. Employers — increasingly including Chinese and international mining operators — cannot find locally trained technicians at the skill level they require.

Why IEG's approach works here

IEG's equipment is designed for industrial training environments, not university labs — robust, modular, and serviceable. The hybrid power configuration (solar-assisted) directly addresses load-shedding. The AI-assisted delivery model with offline capability means training continues even when connectivity fails. TEVETA's willingness to co-accredit with Korean SKKNI standards creates an internationally portable qualification that mining operators recognise.

Specific interventions
  • Deploy IEG 3200-M (PLC/automation) and IEG 6030 (mechatronics) at one TEVETA-accredited college
  • Solar-assisted hybrid power supply integrated into Living Lab setup
  • Offline-capable AI curriculum delivery via Emotii platform (English + Nyanja/Bemba)
  • Co-author TEVETA Level 4 Electrical/Mechanical Technician programme with local TVET experts
  • Direct employer MOU with 2–3 copper processing or manufacturing operators for graduate intake
  • Instructor certification: 10 trainers through adapted IEG Train-the-Trainer programme

Our Approach

Three Pillars, One Integrated Pilot

This initiative is not a technology transfer project. It is a co-designed system where each pillar reinforces the others — local expertise shapes how Korean infrastructure is deployed, and AI-assisted delivery makes both sustainable.

01

Smart Factory Infrastructure

IEG training equipment deployed as a Living Lab within existing TVET institutions.

02

AI-Assisted Pedagogy

Multilingual AI tutoring layer (Emotii) that adapts to learner pace and local language context.

03

Co-Designed Curriculum

NQF/TEVETA-aligned curriculum built jointly with local TVET experts and industry employers.

Smart Factory Infrastructure — What Gets Deployed

  • IEG 3200-M Smart Factory Trainer (PLC + HMI + pneumatics integration)
  • IEG 3432 Electrical/Electronics workstation for industrial diagnostics
  • IEG 6030 Mechatronics training system with sensor integration
  • Hybrid power configuration (solar + grid) for load-shedding resilience
  • Remote monitoring dashboard for equipment health and utilisation tracking
  • On-site installation + 2-year maintenance agreement included

AI-Assisted Pedagogy — How It Works

  • Emotii AI platform: multilingual content delivery (English + local language support)
  • Adaptive assessment engine that identifies knowledge gaps in real time
  • Instructor dashboard: tracks cohort progress, flags struggling learners
  • Offline-capable mode for low-bandwidth and unreliable connectivity environments
  • AI-generated simulations supplement physical equipment when unavailable
  • Content library co-authored with local TVET instructors during pilot phase

Co-Designed Curriculum — The Partnership Process

  • Joint curriculum workshop: IEG technical team + local TVET experts (3-day intensive)
  • Industry demand mapping with manufacturing employers in each pilot country
  • NQF Level 4 / TEVETA Level 4 qualification pathway designed from the outset
  • BTEC unit mapping for international portability of qualifications
  • Local experts hold Co-PI status — equal decision-making authority in design
  • Six-month review cycle with employer satisfaction and employment rate tracking
